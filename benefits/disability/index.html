Disability


A disabling injury or illness, or even the birth of a child, that keeps you out of work could have a devastating impact on your income, jeopardizing your ability to cover normal household expenses. With the right disability coverage, your income is protected, relieving you of the anxiety of depleting your savings to pay your bills.

SHORT TERM DISABILITY

Short Term Disability replaces a portion of your income if an injury, illness or maternity forces you out of work for an extended period of time. HBC provides basic short term disability coverage at no cost to you, and enrollment is automatic, provided you are eligible.

Who is Eligible: All full-time Associates working 30+ hours become elgible as follows:

  • Salaried Associates are eligible the first of the month following 60 days of service;
  • Full-Time Hourly Associates are eligible the first of the month following 6 months of service.

If eligible, you may receive pay for up to 26 weeks, depending on your approved medical necessity and prior medical disability leaves taken within the immediately preceding 12 months. Your pay while on an approved medical leave will be based on the following charts, however, prior leaves taken during the immediately preceding 12 months will reduce the pay weeks remaining.


Benefit Amount Duration
Salaried Associates 100% of your weekly earnings Up to the first 8 weeks of disability
75% of your weekly earnings Up to the next 8 weeks of disability
50% of your weekly earnings Up to the next 10 weeks of disability
Benefit Amount Duration
Full-Time Hourly Associates 60% of your weekly earnings Benefits begin on the 8th day after illness or injury (following the 7-day elimination period*) and continue for up to 25 weeks

NOTE: There is no elimination period for hospitalization.


In order to receive salary continuation pay while on medical disability, you must contact Cigna Disability to report your disability.

LONG TERM DISABILITY INSURANCE

HBC also offers Long Term Disability (LTD) insurance to protect your finances when your disability continues beyond the period covered by the Short Term Disability plan.


Who is Eligible: All full-time Associates working 30+ hours become eligible as follows:

  • Salaried Associates are eligible the first of the month following 60 days of service;
  • Full-Time Hourly Associates are eligible the first of the month following 6 months of service.

Salaried Associates

HBC provides Salaried Associates with a core LTD benefit at NO COST to you. You can also enroll in the buy-up LTD plan, which pays an additional 20% of your monthly earnings. See the chart below for benefit amount information on each plan. You pay the cost difference of the buy-up benefit through convenient payroll deductions. You also have the option to pay taxes on the company-paid LTD premium to allow a tax-free benefit in the event you become disabled. Rates are based on your age as of January 1 each year.


Full-Time Hourly Associates

Hourly Associates can choose to enroll in the core or buy-up LTD coverage. See the chart below for benefit amount information on each plan. You pay the cost of these benefits through convenient payroll deductions. Rates are based on your age as of January 1 each year.

Plan Benefit Amount
Core LTD You will receive 40% of your monthly covered earnings, up to a maximum of $12,500 per month ($100 minimum or 10% of monthly benefit).
Buy-Up LTD You will receive 60% of your monthly earnings, up to a maximum of $12,500 per month ($100 minimum or 10% of monthly benefit).

Long Term Disability Definition of Earnings

For the purpose of Long Term Disability premium calculations, the general definition of earnings for:

  • Salaried Associates is annual base pay
  • Full-time Hourly Associates is base hourly rate multiplied by 38.5 hours and multiplied again by 52 weeks (plus commissions, if applicable)

The premium calculations reflect the pay period immediately prior to or concurrent with September 1 each year. The premiums will be effective January 1 of each year.


Benefits will be paid based on earnings as of the date of claim.
