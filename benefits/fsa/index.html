Flexible Spending Accounts


Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) allow you to set aside money on a pre-tax basis to pay for qualified out-of-pocket health care or dependent care expenses. Because the money is deducted from your paycheck on a pre-tax basis, you will reduce the amount of your taxable income.

HBC offers a Limited Purpose Health Care FSA, a General Purpose Health Care FSA and a Dependent Care FSA. These accounts are separate – you cannot use money from a Health Care FSA to cover expenses eligible under the Dependent Care FSA or vice versa.

HOW FLEXIBLE SPENDING ACCOUNTS WORK

  1. Each year, you decide how much to set aside for health care and/or dependent care expenses.
  2. Your contributions are deducted from your paycheck on a pre-tax basis in equal installments throughout the calendar year.
  3. As you incur health care or dependent care expenses throughout the year, you’ll need to submit a claim form for reimbursement. Your claim will be processed, and you will be reimbursed from your account. The Health Care FSA also includes a payment card that you can use to pay for eligible expenses at the point of sale. If you use the card to pay for your eligible medical expense, you will not have to file a claim for reimbursement, as you have not had any out of pocket cost. However, you should retain your receipts as you may be required to provide them to substantiate your expenses.
  4. Be sure to calculate your expenses conservatively when making your FSA elections. IRS regulations require that you forfeit any money left in your account after the claims submission deadline. Only claims incurred during the calendar year while covered by the FSA are eligible for reimbursement.
  5. Your full plan year Health Care FSA election is available for reimbursement immediately; only contributions made through payroll deduction are available for use under the dependent care FSA account.
  6. You MUST actively enroll or re-enroll in the FSAs each year. You are NOT automatically re-enrolled.

Below is a table comparing the various FSA's available:

IMPORTANT FSA REMINDERS:

  • Consider your contributions. Be sure to calculate your expenses conservatively when making your FSA elections. IRS regulations require that you forfeit any money left in your account after the claims submissions deadline.


  • Limited Purpose Health Care FSA Reminder. You may wish to consider making contributions to your HSA first. The HSA may also cover dental and vision expenses, and does not have a risk of forfeiture if amounts go unused.


  • FSA with a CDHP Reminder. If you elected a General Purpose Health Care FSA for 2017 and you choose to enroll in an HBC CDHP for 2018, any funds remaining in your 2017 General Purpose Health Care FSA will roll over into a Limited Purpose Health Care FSA for 2018.

ConnectYourCare Benefits

Your Health Care and Dependent Care FSAs are administered by ConnectYourCare (CYC). CYC offers benefits to help you make the most of your FSA funds.

Below are important details about your FSA.


  • Advanced Features – CYC offers online access to your account information, 24-hour customer service support, access to a smartphone mobile app, text message alerts and direct deposit for faster claims reimbursements.
  • Easy Claims Process – Claims should be submitted to CYC online or through the mobile app. You can also snap a picture of your receipt with your phone and upload it to your online account.
  • Payment Card – Pay for services using your payment card, which works like a debit card. It allows you to easily pay for eligible health care expenses at the point of service using your Health Care FSA funds. Your payment card will be mailed to your home address generally by your coverage effective date. Please note, documentation may still be required to substantiate claims, so be sure to keep your itemized receipts for all transactions.

Register Your Account

After you have completed your enrollment, visit the ConnectYourCare website at www.ConnectYourCare.com to register as a new user and set up your online account.

FSA Grace Period

If you are currently enrolled in a 2017 Flexible Spending Account (FSA) you have until March 15, 2018, the Grace Period, to use your funds. If you enroll in an HBC CDHP, your current Health Care FSA (HCFSA) will automatically transition to a Limited Purpose FSA on January 1, 2018, for the Grace Period. The Limited Purpose FSA may only be used for dental and vision expenses. Below are details on your 2017 FSA:

  • Medical expenses must be incurred (services received) by December 31, 2017 to be eligible for reimbursement.
  • Dental and vision expenses may be incurred through March 15, 2018.
  • Dependent care expenses may be incurred through March 15, 2018.
  • Claims for all 2017 expenses may be submitted through June 15, 2018, the Run-out Period.
